jeremiah 2:13 NLT

13 For my people have done two evil things: They have abandoned me--the fountain of living water. And they have dug themselves cracked cisterns that can hold no water at all!

one of the prime symbols in the scripture canons is the fountain of living waters, where anybody can come and draw endless power and strength. fountains are known for their consistent and perennial flow and abundance, as opposed to cisterns where their capacity have a limit and the water can dry up and turn stale. to further clarify this symbol, i'll pull two scripture verses from the NT and BOM.

revelation 7:17

17 For the Lamb which is in the midst of the throne shall feed them, and shall lead them unto living fountains of waters: and God shall wipe away all tears from their eyes.

in this verse, we learn that jesus christ is the path that leads toward this fountain.

1 nephi 11:25

25 And it came to pass that I beheld that the rod of iron, which my father had seen, was the word of God, which led to the fountain of living waters, or to the tree of life; which waters are a representation of the love of God; and I also beheld that the tree of life was a representation of the love of God.

and in this verse, we learn that this fountain is the love of god which we know is unconditional and eternal.

so israel made two wrong moves that ultimately led to the destruction of jerusalem. the word "abandoned" in jeremiah 2:13 implies that the love and favor of the lord towards israel never departed nor ceased, and the part where it says "they have dug themselves cracked cisterns" shows that israel was the one who haphazardly turned disloyal and deliberately sought other gods.

let me quote here a sidebar from hank smith in the podcast, follow him:

...the Savior told a great parable about this when he said there was a house that had an evil spirit and they casted the evil spirit out and then it returns with seven of its friends because the house wasn't full of anything else. The sins returned with even more sins, with even more friends...

this is the great danger in trying to shift from a mindset/behavior to another. this is essentially what norman v. peale emphasized since it's such a simple error to make: while you're attempting tyou cao eradicate negative thought patterns in your brain, you can't leave it vacant. immediately, you fill it with positive thought patterns; otherwise, it becomes an empty vessel into which wrong thoughts might enter and inhabit.

this is almost comparable to israel's futile attempts to jump on two branches, thinking they can get away with it in the eyes the one true god.
